Who is Stevie Wonder?

Stevie Wonder is a legendary American musician, singer, and songwriter, known for his groundbreaking contributions to soul, pop, and R&B. Born in 1950 and blind since infancy, he became a child prodigy signed to Motown Records at 11. With iconic hits like "Superstition" and "Isn't She Lovely," Wonder is celebrated for his rich vocals, instrumental mastery, and social activism.

That's cool but what more did he do?

Stevie Wonder transformed music with his groundbreaking use of synthesizers and multi-track recording, shaping the sound of modern R&B and pop. He released landmark albums like Songs in the Key of Life and Innervisions and wrote influential songs for social change, including "Happy Birthday," which supported the campaign to establish Martin Luther King Jr. Day. He also used his platform to advocate for racial equality, disability rights, and humanitarian causes worldwide.

Stevie Wonder remains active in music and social advocacy. In 2024, he released a new song, embarked on a tour promoting unity, and performed at the Democratic National Convention.
